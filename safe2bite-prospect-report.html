Issues & Gaps

Safe2Bite: Zero paid ad infrastructure

No Google Ads, no Meta Pixel, no conversion tracking of any kind. The website is receiving only organic and direct traffic. There is no mechanism to measure or grow paid acquisition for the Safe2Bite brand.

Neither brand has Meta Pixel installed

No Facebook or Instagram advertising infrastructure exists on either site. No retargeting audiences are being built. The food allergy and OIT category on Meta is completely uncontested in North Texas — any competitor who installs a pixel now will own that audience pool first.

TexasAllergyMD tracks phone calls only, not form submissions

The Google Ads conversion tag fires sitewide for phone calls. There is no separate event firing when a user submits the appointment request form. This means Google's algorithm is optimising towards callers only, potentially excluding high-intent patients who prefer to book online.

No dedicated landing pages for ad campaigns

Google Ads traffic goes to standard site pages. There is no controlled environment for ad-to-page message match, headline testing, or isolating paid conversion rates. This also means the practice cannot A/B test offer framing or calls to action specifically for ad traffic.

No Bing Ads on either site

Microsoft Search Ads (Bing) typically deliver 15 to 25% lower CPCs than Google for medical and healthcare search terms, while reaching a largely non-overlapping audience. Neither site has a Bing UET tag installed.

TexasAllergyMD still firing a deprecated Universal Analytics tag

UA-231588930-11 is still in the page head. Google sunset Universal Analytics in July 2023 — this tag sends no data and creates unnecessary page load. It should be removed.

Opportunity: GTM is already installed on both sites

Both sites use Google Tag Manager. Meta Pixel, Google Ads conversion events, form submission tracking, and remarketing audiences can all be deployed through GTM without touching the website code. Setup time is hours, not weeks.
